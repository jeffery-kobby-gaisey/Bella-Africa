About Ghana

Ghana, located in West Africa, is known for its rich culture and growing economy. However, rural areas still face significant challenges in education, healthcare, and economic opportunities, especially for women.

  • Population: 31 million
  • Rural Population: 43%
  • Women in Workforce: 67%
  • Literacy Rate: 79%

Women's Challenges

Women in rural Ghana face multiple barriers to economic empowerment, including limited access to education, financial resources, and market opportunities.

  • Limited access to formal employment
  • Gender wage gap of 30%
  • Limited access to financial services
  • Traditional gender roles

Sustainable Development

Our approach focuses on sustainable solutions that create lasting impact through skills development, community engagement, and economic empowerment.

  • Skills-based training programs
  • Community-led initiatives
  • Long-term capacity building
  • Local partnership development

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the main challenges women face in rural Ghana?

Women in rural Ghana face multiple challenges including limited access to education, financial resources, formal employment opportunities, and traditional gender roles that limit their economic participation.

How does Bella Africa measure its impact?

We measure our impact through various metrics including the number of women empowered, income generated, students benefited, lives transformed, and long-term community development indicators.

What makes Bella Africa's approach sustainable?

Our approach focuses on skills development, community engagement, local partnership development, and long-term capacity building rather than short-term aid.

How can I learn more about specific projects?

You can learn more about our projects through our project reports, videos, impact data, and by contacting us directly for detailed information about specific initiatives.
